## By Audience

### Students / PhD Candidates

#### NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP)
- **Funder:** National Science Foundation (USA)
- **URL:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program
- **Amount:** $37,000/year stipend + $16,000 cost of education allowance for 3 years
- **Eligibility:** US citizens/nationals/permanent residents; undergraduate seniors or early graduate students in STEM
- **Deadline:** Field-specific, typically mid-to-late October
- **Description:** NSF's flagship program supporting outstanding graduate students in research-based STEM degrees. Portable fellowship established in 1952.
- **Special:** ~2,000 awards from ~14,000 applications annually; includes $4,000 research/travel account

#### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
- **Funder:** Tri-agency (NSERC, CIHR, SSHRC), Canada
- **URL:** https://www.canada.ca/en/institutes-health-research/news/2024/05/celebrating-excellence-in-canadian-research
- **Amount:** CAD $50,000/year for 3 years
- **Eligibility:** Doctoral students; must be nominated by Canadian institution; open to Canadian and international students
- **Deadline:** Institutional nomination required
- **Description:** Supports world-class doctoral students demonstrating academic excellence, research potential, and leadership across all disciplines.
- **Special:** Final year of program (being replaced by new consolidated talent program); 166 scholarships awarded annually

#### Google PhD Fellowship Program
- **Funder:** Google
- **URL:** https://research.google/programs-and-events/phd-fellowship/
- **Amount:** US/Canada: $85,000/year for up to 2 years; varies by region
- **Eligibility:** PhD students at eligible universities worldwide; must be nominated by university
- **Deadline:** April-May annually
- **Description:** Supports exceptional PhD students in computer architecture, algorithms, health research, HCI, machine learning, NLP, quantum computing, and more.
- **Special:** Google Research Mentor assigned; internship opportunities

#### NIH Individual Predoctoral Fellowship (F31)
- **Funder:** National Institutes of Health (USA)
- **URL:** https://researchtraining.nih.gov/programs/fellowships
- **Amount:** Annual stipend + up to $16,000/year tuition/fees + institutional allowance
- **Eligibility:** US citizens/nationals/permanent residents; enrolled in PhD or equivalent; at dissertation stage
- **Deadline:** April 8, August 8, December 8 (non-AIDS)
- **Description:** Ruth L. Kirschstein NRSA supporting up to 5 years aggregate predoctoral training for dissertation research in health-related fields.
- **Special:** Must be at dissertation stage; fellowship to individual, not institution

#### Microsoft Research PhD Fellowship
- **Funder:** Microsoft
- **URL:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/academic-program/
- **Amount:** Covers tuition and fees for 2 years + $42,000 annual stipend
- **Eligibility:** PhD students at North American universities; must be in third year of PhD when applying
- **Deadline:** Department chair submissions typically October
- **Description:** Supports PhD students in systems \u0026 networking, AI, ML, computer vision, and robotics closely related to Microsoft Research topics.
- **Special:** Increased stipend by nearly 25% in recent years

#### JSPS Postdoctoral Fellowship (for PhD students)
- **Funder:** Japan Society for the Promotion of Science
- **URL:** https://www.jsps.go.jp/english/e-fellow/
- **Amount:** JPY 362,000/month + travel + settling-in allowance + research grant
- **Eligibility:** PhD students and holders from any country
- **Deadline:** Multiple rounds per year
- **Description:** Supports 1-24 month cooperative research stays with leading research groups in Japanese universities.
- **Special:** Standard (12-24 months) and Short-term (1-12 months) programs available

### Postdocs / Early Career Researchers

#### NIH Pathway to Independence Award (K99/R00)
- **Funder:** National Institutes of Health (USA)
- **URL:** https://researchtraining.nih.gov/programs/career-development/K99-R00
- **Amount:** K99: up to $90,000/year; R00: up to $249,000/year total costs
- **Eligibility:** ≤4 years postdoc experience at application; in mentored postdoctoral position
- **Deadline:** February, June, October (standard NIH dates)
- **Description:** Two-phase award: 1-2 years mentored training (K99), then up to 3 years independent research (R00) after securing tenure-track position.
- **Special:** Must complete ≥12 months K99 phase; must secure tenure-track position to activate R00

#### Canada Postdoctoral Research Award (CPRA)
- **Funder:** Tri-agency (CIHR, NSERC, SSHRC), Canada
- **URL:** https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PD-NP/cpra-bprc_eng.asp
- **Amount:** CAD $70,000/year for 2 years
- **Eligibility:** Top postdoctoral applicants nationally and internationally; must apply to Canadian institution
- **Deadline:** Annual competition
- **Description:** Harmonized postdoctoral fellowship program for exceptional applicants. Replaces the former Banting Postdoctoral Fellowships program.
- **Special:** International applicants eligible

#### ERC Starting Grant
- **Funder:** European Research Council
- **URL:** https://erc.europa.eu/apply-grant/starting-grant
- **Amount:** €1.5 million standard (5 years); up to €3.5 million for those relocating to Europe
- **Eligibility:** 2-7 years post-PhD; any nationality; must conduct research in EU/Associated Country
- **Deadline:** Annual call, typically October
- **Description:** Supports talented early-career researchers ready to work independently and establish their own research teams.
- **Special:** Bottom-up approach in any research field; 28 evaluation panels across 3 domains

#### Royal Society University Research Fellowships (URF)
- **Funder:** Royal Society (UK)
- **URL:** https://royalsociety.org/grants/university-research/
- **Amount:** Up to £1.87M over 8 years; 80% FEC
- **Eligibility:** 3-8 years post-PhD; UK/Ireland; any nationality; natural sciences; no permanent post
- **Deadline:** Annual (September)
- **Description:** 8-year fellowships supporting outstanding theoretical scientists. Covers salary, expenses, PhD studentships.
- **Special:** Up to 35 awards; host commits to open-ended position

#### ARC Discovery Early Career Researcher Award (DECRA)
- **Funder:** Australian Research Council
- **URL:** https://www.arc.gov.au/funding-research/funding-schemes/discovery-program/discovery-early-career-researcher-award-decra
- **Amount:** Up to AUD $176,693/year for 3 years (~AUD $530,000 total)
- **Eligibility:** PhD awarded on/after March 1, 2021 (with career interruptions); must dedicate 80%+ time to research
- **Deadline:** Typically March
- **Description:** Supports outstanding early-career researchers with demonstrated capacity for high-quality research and emerging leadership.
- **Special:** International eligibility; up to 200 awards per round

#### Schmidt Science Fellows
- **Funder:** Schmidt Futures (partnership with Rhodes Trust)
- **URL:** https://schmidtsciencefellows.org/
- **Amount:** Up to $220,000 over 1-2 years ($110,000/year personal stipend)
- **Eligibility:** PhD expected May 2025-June 2026; natural sciences, engineering, mathematics, computing; institutional nomination from partner universities
- **Deadline:** Nomination deadlines vary by institution (typically April-May)
- **Description:** Postdoctoral program for interdisciplinary scientific leadership. Must pivot to different discipline from PhD field.
- **Special:** 32 fellows annually; Science Leadership Program included

#### Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships
- **Funder:** European Commission (Horizon Europe)
- **URL:** https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/actions/postdoctoral-fellowships
- **Amount:** Living allowance + mobility allowance + research costs; typically €200,000-€300,000 total
- **Eligibility:** Must hold PhD at deadline; maximum 8 years post-PhD experience; mobility requirement
- **Deadline:** Annual call, typically September
- **Description:** European Fellowships (1-2 years in Europe) and Global Fellowships (with 1 year mandatory return to Europe).
- **Special:** 2025 call received record 17,058 proposals; optional 6-month non-academic placement

#### Humboldt Research Fellowship (Postdocs)
- **Funder:** Alexander von Humboldt Foundation (Germany)
- **URL:** https://www.humboldt-foundation.de/en/apply/sponsorship-programmes/humboldt-research-fellowship
- **Amount:** €2,700/month + travel, family allowances, health insurance
- **Eligibility:** Doctorate completed within last 4 years; non-German nationals or Germans abroad 5+ years
- **Deadline:** Continuous; selection committee meets March, July, November
- **Description:** Supports researchers of all nationalities for 6-24 month research stays at German institutions.
- **Special:** 20-25% approval rate; flexible starting date; hosts receive research cost subsidy

### Faculty / Principal Investigators

#### NSF Faculty Early Career Development Program (CAREER)
- **Funder:** National Science Foundation (USA)
- **URL:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/career-faculty-early-career-development-program
- **Amount:** Minimum $400,000-$500,000 over 5 years
- **Eligibility:** Untenured, tenure-track assistant professors; typically within 5-7 years of PhD; no prior CAREER award
- **Deadline:** Annual, fourth Wednesday in July
- **Description:** NSF's most prestigious award for early-career faculty integrating research and education.
- **Special:** Up to 26 awardees selected for Presidential Early Career Awards (PECASE); Foundation-wide

#### NIH Research Project Grant (R01)
- **Funder:** National Institutes of Health (USA)
- **URL:** https://grants.nih.gov/funding/activity-codes/R01
- **Amount:** Generally unlimited but must reflect project needs; \u003e$500K direct costs requires prior approval
- **Eligibility:** Open to domestic/foreign institutions; all career stages; no citizenship requirement
- **Deadline:** February 5, June 5, October 5 (standard dates)
- **Description:** NIH's original and oldest grant mechanism for health-related research; typically 1-5 year awards.
- **Special:** Modular budget for domestic organizations; renewable; considered NIH "gold standard"

#### ERC Consolidator Grant
- **Funder:** European Research Council
- **URL:** https://erc.europa.eu/apply-grant/consolidator-grant
- **Amount:** €2 million standard (5 years); up to €4 million for those relocating to Europe
- **Eligibility:** 7-12 years post-PhD; any nationality; must conduct research in EU/Associated Country
- **Deadline:** Annual call
- **Description:** Supports excellent researchers consolidating their own independent research teams.
- **Special:** 2024: 328 grants totaling €678 million; 39% to women (highest ever)

#### DOE Office of Science Early Career Research Program
- **Funder:** U.S. Department of Energy, Office of Science
- **URL:** https://science.osti.gov/early-career
- **Amount:** Universities: ~$875,000 over 5 years; National labs: ~$2,750,000 over 5 years
- **Eligibility:** Untenured tenure-track faculty OR DOE lab/user facility employee; within 10 years of PhD
- **Deadline:** Pre-applications late January/early February; full applications April
- **Description:** Supports individual research programs across eight Office of Science areas.
- **Special:** Over 80 awards annually; encouragement phase before full application

#### UKRI Future Leaders Fellowships
- **Funder:** UKRI (cross-council), UK
- **URL:** https://www.ukri.org/what-we-do/developing-people-and-skills/future-leaders-fellowships/
- **Amount:** £300K-£2M+ (no limit); 80% FEC
- **Eligibility:** Early career transitioning to independence; UK-based; all nationalities; all fields
- **Deadline:** Rolling rounds
- **Description:** 4+3 year fellowships enabling development of research themes and team building.
- **Special:** Flexible working; host commits to open-ended position; ~500 awards 2018-2021

#### Sloan Research Fellowships
- **Funder:** Alfred P. Sloan Foundation
- **URL:** https://sloan.org/fellowships/
- **Amount:** $75,000 over 2 years
- **Eligibility:** Early-career faculty (tenure-track, untenured) at U.S. or Canadian institutions; PhD in chemistry, CS, Earth system science, economics, mathematics, neuroscience, or physics
- **Deadline:** Nominations typically due September 15
- **Description:** Recognizes and rewards outstanding early-career faculty with potential to revolutionize their fields.
- **Special:** 118-128 fellowships annually; nomination-based; no indirect costs; 53 fellows have won Nobel Prize since 1955

#### Packard Fellowships for Science and Engineering
- **Funder:** David and Lucile Packard Foundation
- **URL:** https://www.packard.org/approach/fellowships-for-science-engineering/
- **Amount:** $875,000 over 5 years ($175,000/year)
- **Eligibility:** Within first 3 years of first faculty appointment; tenure-track at one of 50 invited U.S. universities
- **Deadline:** Internal deadlines vary; external typically March 15
- **Description:** Unrestricted funding for early-career researchers pursuing blue-sky thinking.
- **Special:** 20 fellowships annually; 50% of 2022 class were women; presidential nomination required

#### HHMI Investigators
- **Funder:** Howard Hughes Medical Institute
- **URL:** https://www.hhmi.org/programs/investigators
- **Amount:** Approximately $11 million over 7-year renewable term (~$1.6 million/year)
- **Eligibility:** Tenure-track/tenured faculty at eligible U.S. institutions; 5-15 years post-training; must dedicate 75% time to research
- **Deadline:** Next competition 2027 (opens every 3 years)
- **Description:** Investigators become HHMI employees while maintaining university appointments. "People, not projects" approach.
- **Special:** ~25 investigators every 3 years; 30+ current/former investigators have won Nobel Prizes

### Open-Source Software Maintainers

#### GitHub Sponsors
- **Funder:** GitHub (Microsoft)
- **URL:** https://github.com/sponsors
- **Amount:** Variable; no fixed limits, 100% of funds go to recipients
- **Eligibility:** Anyone contributing to OSS (code, documentation, leadership, etc.); must be in supported region
- **Deadline:** Rolling
- **Description:** Platform allowing individuals and organizations to financially support OSS developers through recurring or one-time payments.
- **Special:** $5,000 matching fund in first year; GitHub Secure OSS Fund ($1.25M, $10K per project) for security

#### Sovereign Tech Fund
- **Funder:** Sovereign Tech Agency (German Federal Ministry)
- **URL:** https://www.sovereign.tech/programs/fund
- **Amount:** €50,000 minimum, up to €1M+ (no fixed upper limit)
- **Eligibility:** Global; open digital base technologies with OSI/FSF-approved licenses
- **Deadline:** Rolling basis; ~6 months from application to decision
- **Description:** German public funding for open source infrastructure critical to digital sovereignty. Funded 60+ projects with €24.6M since 2022.
- **Special:** Project managers assigned; payment by milestone; includes Bug Resilience Program

#### NLnet Foundation
- **Funder:** NLnet Foundation (Netherlands)
- **URL:** https://nlnet.nl
- **Amount:** €5,000 to €50,000 per project
- **Eligibility:** Global; projects with open licenses (OSI/FSF-approved); focus on internet infrastructure, privacy, security
- **Deadline:** Bimonthly calls
- **Description:** Dutch foundation funding internet technologies since 1997. Funded 400+ projects including Mastodon, Peertube, Wireguard.
- **Special:** Fast decision-making; flexible; supports projects from idea to implementation

#### NumFOCUS Small Development Grants
- **Funder:** NumFOCUS (501(c)(3) nonprofit)
- **URL:** https://numfocus.org/programs/small-development-grants
- **Amount:** Up to $10,000 per proposal; max 2 grants per project annually
- **Eligibility:** NumFOCUS Fiscally Sponsored or Affiliated projects only
- **Deadline:** Three annual cycles (Spring, Summer, Fall)
- **Description:** Supports scientific computing OSS projects with development, maintenance, community growth. ~$60,000 annually.
- **Special:** Focus on usability, community growth, major releases; transparent review via GitHub

#### Prototype Fund
- **Funder:** Open Knowledge Foundation Germany (BMBF-funded)
- **URL:** https://www.prototypefund.de/en
- **Amount:** €95,000 (6 months) or €158,000 (10 months) per project
- **Eligibility:** Individuals/small teams in Germany; must be FOSS with added social value
- **Deadline:** Annual call, October 1 - November 30
- **Description:** Low-threshold German funding for civic tech, data literacy, data security, and software infrastructure. ~400 projects funded with €16.6M total.
- **Special:** Coaching included; focus on data security/infrastructure (2025+)

#### Python Software Foundation Grants
- **Funder:** Python Software Foundation
- **URL:** https://www.python.org/psf/grants/
- **Amount:** Typically ~$10 per conference attendee for events; variable for other grants
- **Eligibility:** Python-related projects, conferences, workshops, communities globally
- **Deadline:** Rolling (when active)
- **Description:** **TEMPORARILY PAUSED** (hit 2025 funding cap). Disbursed record $952,000 to 174 groups across 52 countries in 2024.
- **Special:** Also provides fiscal sponsorship; travel grants for PyCon

### Indie Builders / Microgrants

#### Emergent Ventures
- **Funder:** Mercatus Center at George Mason University
- **URL:** https://www.mercatus.org/emergent-ventures
- **Amount:** $1,000 - $50,000 (varies widely)
- **Eligibility:** Age 13+; global applications
- **Deadline:** Rolling; ~1,500 word proposal
- **Description:** Fellowship/grant program for "zero to one" ideas advancing prosperity, opportunity, liberty, well-being. Administered by Tyler Cowen.
- **Special:** Fast decisions; multiple verticals (main, EV India, EV AI); annual unconference for grantees

#### Awesome Foundation
- **Funder:** Autonomous local chapters
- **URL:** https://www.awesomefoundation.org/en
- **Amount:** $1,000 per grant
- **Eligibility:** Anyone globally; individuals, groups, organizations
- **Deadline:** Monthly grants by chapter
- **Description:** 66 chapters across 9 countries. Each autonomous chapter gives monthly $1,000 grants for "awesome" projects. 7,458 projects funded ($7.458M total).
- **Special:** Completely autonomous chapters; micro-trustees pool $100 each; no equity taken

#### 1517 Fund - Medici Project
- **Funder:** 1517 Fund
- **URL:** https://www.1517fund.com/medici
- **Amount:** Minimum $1,000 (no-strings-attached grant); potential for larger VC investment
- **Eligibility:** High school/college students and dropouts globally
- **Deadline:** Rolling; 5-minute Loom video via email
- **Description:** Supports young makers (3,000+ grants given). Alumni include Vitalik Buterin, Dylan Field (Figma).
- **Special:** No equity taken on grants; Venmo payment; Discord community; focus on makers

#### Unitary Fund Microgrants
- **Funder:** Unitary Fund (quantum computing nonprofit)
- **URL:** https://unitary.foundation
- **Amount:** $4,000 per project (previously $2,000)
- **Eligibility:** Anyone globally; quantum technology projects
- **Deadline:** Rolling; simple form + 2-minute video
- **Description:** No-strings-attached grants for quantum computing OSS. 100+ microgrants awarded across 23 countries.
- **Special:** No organizational affiliation required; 87% of recipients get additional resources

## By Region

### Global Grants

#### OpenAI Researcher Access Program
- **Funder:** OpenAI
- **URL:** https://openai.com/form/researcher-access-program/
- **Amount:** Up to $1,000 in API credits (12-month validity); some research supported under $25k
- **Eligibility:** Researchers with active affiliation to academic institution/research organization; especially those with limited resources
- **Deadline:** Rolling; reviewed quarterly (March, June, September, December)
- **Description:** Supports alignment, fairness, societal impact, and interdisciplinary research on AI safety and responsible AI deployment.
- **Special:** Credits expire after 1 year; bound by usage policies

#### Fulbright U.S. Student Program
- **Funder:** Fulbright Commission / US Department of State
- **URL:** https://us.fulbrightonline.org/
- **Amount:** Varies by country; typically full funding or travel grant
- **Eligibility:** US citizens; graduating seniors, graduate students, young professionals
- **Deadline:** October (for following academic year)
- **Description:** Study and research grants worldwide (160+ countries). Most competitive and prestigious international exchange.
- **Special:** Full support or travel grants; extensive cultural exchange component

#### Bill \u0026 Melinda Gates Foundation - Grand Challenges
- **Funder:** Bill \u0026 Melinda Gates Foundation
- **URL:** https://www.gatesfoundation.org
- **Amount:** Grand Challenges: up to $20 million; Explorations: $100,000 initial + up to $1 million follow-on
- **Eligibility:** Organizations worldwide; all career levels
- **Deadline:** Rolling/periodic depending on specific challenge
- **Description:** Supports innovative global health and development research, focusing on diseases affecting developing countries.
- **Special:** Partnerships with NIH, Wellcome Trust, CIHR; emphasis on deliverable technologies

### United States

#### NSF CAREER (see Faculty section)

#### NIH R01 (see Faculty section)

#### DARPA Young Faculty Award (YFA)
- **Funder:** Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency
- **URL:** https://www.darpa.mil/work-with-us/young-faculty-award

- **Eligibility:** Junior faculty at U.S. institutions or non-profit research institutions; those without prior DARPA funding especially encouraged

- **Funder:** DOD Basic Research Office (managed by ONR)
- **URL:** https://basicresearch.defense.gov/

- **Eligibility:** Generally ~20 years post-PhD; impressive credentials; U.S. academic institutions
- **Deadline:** Annual (170 white papers → 27 proposals → ~11 fellows in FY2024)
- **Description:** DOD's most prestigious single-investigator award for basic research addressing science/engineering topics of DOD interest.
- **Special:** For exceptional senior researchers; sustained cutting-edge fundamental research

#### NEH Fellowships
- **Funder:** National Endowment for the Humanities
- **URL:** https://www.neh.gov/grants/research/fellowships
- **Amount:** $5,000/month; 6-12 months; maximum $60,000 for 12 months
- **Eligibility:** U.S. citizens or foreign nationals residing in U.S. ≥3 years; researchers, teachers, writers
- **Deadline:** Typically April for following year
- **Description:** Competitive awards for exceptional humanities research, rigorous analysis, and clear writing projects.
- **Special:** ~80 recipients/year from ~$4.8M budget

#### MacArthur Fellows Program ("Genius Grants")
- **Funder:** John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation
- **URL:** https://www.macfound.org/programs/awards/fellows/
- **Amount:** $800,000 over 5 years (no strings attached)
- **Eligibility:** U.S. citizens or residents; any field, any career stage; cannot apply (nomination-based, confidential)
- **Deadline:** Not applicable
- **Description:** Invests in creative individuals showing "extraordinary originality and dedication." 20-30 fellows annually.
- **Special:** Completely unrestricted; anonymous nomination; 1,175 fellows since 1981

### European Union

#### ERC Starting, Consolidator, Advanced, and Synergy Grants (see earlier sections)

#### MSCA Doctoral Networks
- **Funder:** European Commission (Horizon Europe)
- **URL:** https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/actions/doctoral-networks
- **Amount:** €3-4 million per network; 2025 call budget: €597.8 million total
- **Eligibility:** Consortia of minimum 3 organizations from 3 different countries
- **Deadline:** 2025 call opened May 28, closes November 25
- **Description:** Three types: Standard, Industrial (industry-academia co-supervision), and Joint (joint/multiple degrees). Trains multiple doctoral candidates per network.
- **Special:** 2024 awarded 149 programmes (€608.6M) training 1800+ candidates

#### EIC Pathfinder
- **Funder:** European Innovation Council
- **URL:** https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-pathfinder
- **Amount:** €3-4 million per project; 2026 budget: €166M (Open) + €96M (Challenges)
- **Eligibility:** Consortia of minimum 3 entities from different EU/Associated Countries
- **Deadline:** Continuous/multiple cut-offs
- **Description:** Supports early-stage breakthrough research. Pathfinder Open (any field); Pathfinder Challenges (specific themes).
- **Special:** 2025 awarded 44 projects over €140M; booster grants up to €50K for commercialization testing

#### EIC Accelerator
- **Funder:** European Innovation Council / EIC Fund
- **URL:** https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-accelerator
- **Amount:** Grants up to €2.5M + equity €0.5-15M (blended); STEP Scale-up: up to €30M
- **Eligibility:** Start-ups and SMEs in EU/Associated Countries
- **Deadline:** Continuous submission, cut-offs every 2-3 months
- **Description:** For start-ups/SMEs to develop and scale up game-changing innovations with market-creating potential.
- **Special:** Oct 2024: 71 companies selected from 1,211 proposals; 79% opted for blended finance; EIC Fund attracts 3x additional investment

#### Horizon Europe Cluster 4: Digital, Industry and Space
- **Funder:** European Commission
- **URL:** https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/funding/funding-opportunities/funding-programmes-and-open-calls/horizon-europe/cluster-4-digital-industry-and-space
- **Amount:** €2-15M typical per project; 2023-24 WP: €3.7B for digital technologies
- **Eligibility:** Consortia from multiple countries
- **Deadline:** 2025 work programme published May 2025; multiple deadlines
- **Description:** Europe shaping competitive and trusted technologies: AI, quantum, cybersecurity, data, advanced manufacturing, space.
- **Special:** Strong emphasis on engineering, CS, AI; 33.6% of 2023-24 funds contribute to digital transition

### Application Tips

1. **Start early:** 6-12 months for major grants, 3-6 months for travel grants
2. **Join societies:** Many grants require membership ($50-$200/year for students)
3. **Network:** Attend conferences, meet program officers
4. **Strong proposals:** Have reviewed by mentors and colleagues
5. **Follow guidelines precisely:** Formatting, page limits, required documents
6. **Multiple applications:** Many programs allow concurrent applications
7. **Budget realistically:** Justify all costs thoroughly
8. **Diversity statements:** Articulate commitment and impact clearly
9. **Letters of recommendation:** Give writers ample time (4-6 weeks)
10. **Resubmit if not funded:** Many societies allow resubmission with revisions

### Typical Funding Ranges

- **Undergraduate travel:** $250-$1,000
- **Graduate travel:** $500-$2,000
- **Postdoctoral fellowships:** $50,000-$100,000/year
- **Early career faculty:** $400,000-$2,000,000 total over 3-5 years
- **Established investigators:** $500,000-$5,000,000+ over 5 years
- **Microgrants:** $500-$10,000
- **OSS grants:** $5,000-$100,000
