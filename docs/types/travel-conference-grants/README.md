# Travel & Conference Grants

**11 funding opportunities**

*Support for attending conferences, workshops, research visits, and collaborative meetings.*

---

## Overview

Travel grants help researchers attend conferences, present work, network with colleagues, and engage in collaborative research visits. These are particularly valuable for students and early-career researchers building their networks.

### Key Features
- **Amount**: $500 - $3,000 typical range
- **Coverage**: Airfare, registration, accommodation
- **Timeline**: Often rapid decisions (2-8 weeks)
- **Requirements**: Usually need accepted paper/poster or invitation
- **Audience**: All career stages, especially students

## Application Tips

### Eligibility Requirements

**Common Criteria**
- Accepted paper/poster at conference
- Student or early-career status
- Institution affiliation
- Citizenship/residency requirements
- First-time attendee priority (some grants)

**Documentation Needed**
- Acceptance letter from conference
- Abstract/paper submission
- Budget breakdown
- Letter from advisor/supervisor
- Proof of registration
- CV/resume

### Timeline

**Advance Planning**
1. **6-9 months before**: Submit conference paper
2. **3-6 months before**: Receive acceptance, apply for grants
3. **2-4 months before**: Receive funding decisions
4. **1-2 months before**: Book travel, register
5. **After conference**: Submit reimbursement or reports

**Quick Turnaround Grants**
- Some require 45-90 days advance notice
- Others have shorter timelines
- Apply to multiple sources

### Maximizing Success

**Strong Applications**
- **Significance**: Why this conference matters for your research
- **Impact**: How you'll benefit (networking, exposure, collaboration)
- **Presentation**: Highlight accepted paper/talk/poster
- **Budget**: Realistic costs, itemized breakdown
- **Future plans**: How you'll apply what you learn

**Budget Considerations**
- Compare flight options
- Consider conference hotels vs. alternatives
- Account for meals and local transport
- Include registration fees
- Some grants cover partial costs only

## Funding Strategies

### Multiple Applications
- Apply to general and domain-specific grants
- Combine conference-specific and external funding
- Some institutions have internal travel grants
- Professional societies often have member grants

### Stacking Support
- Partial grants can be combined
- Some conferences offer registration waivers
- Advisor/department may supplement
- Student government funding (some universities)

### Cost Reduction
- Share accommodations with other attendees
- Book flights early
- Look for student volunteer opportunities
- Apply for conference scholarships directly
